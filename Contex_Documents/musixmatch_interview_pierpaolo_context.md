## 3. The Role

**Title (functional):** Head of Customer Service / Customer Operations — but deliberately reframed.

**The reframe (central to everything):**
- Not a people-/ops-manager who "manages buttons." Instead: *a builder of the product that runs the next generation of customer service.*
- The target operating model: **instead of hiring 100 people, have ~10 people managing AI agents.** Upskill humans toward managing/orchestrating agents and doing the genuinely human relationship work; automate the rest.
- "Ops exists because product couldn't solve the problem." In the old world, small fixes got pushed behind the roadmap and patched manually. The new mandate is to *build and prevent the problem* instead of staffing around it.

**Team & structure:**
- Reports to Pierpaolo (VP Product). Ops is under Product.
- Manages the existing **customer-support team: 4 people**, who currently report into this position.
- Will also lead a **second, new team from the acquisition** (see §4) — described as possibly ~50 people, of whom only a fraction (~5–10 / ~one third) are retained.

**What "leading two sides" means in practice:**
- **Side A — current team:** already "AI-integrated" (uses Intercom with AI layered on, fed with general app knowledge). Needs to move up a level to "AI-first" — deeper, with per-user memory, more specific and tailored.
- **Side B — acquired team:** no CRM, no process, "more chaotic," email-based, largely unknown to Musixmatch, but high-volume. Must be absorbed quickly with far fewer people; AI must "suck that knowledge" and be able to answer from day one even if the people who used to answer are gone.

**A specific pain point the role is meant to fix (Leo raised it, Pierpaolo agreed):**
- Support knowledge typically never reaches Product → the same problems keep recurring (an information bottleneck). The role should *connect support insight back into product* to close that loop and move faster.

## 4. Strategic Context (the "why")

**Company posture:**
- Musixmatch is ~15 years old and has "always been a tech company" — described as the world's largest lyrics catalog / lyrics-management platform.
- Shifting deliberately from **"AI-integrated" → "AI-first"** in *how they build*. Pierpaolo reports "impressive results" from ~1 year of building this way (new products, new needs solved).

**Tooling trajectory (the proof point for the challenge):**
- They have **already replaced Salesforce** with their own system — reportedly "10× better service" in a few months.
- The stated intent is to do the same to **Intercom**: build a proprietary, AI-first agent-manager / CRM with its own interface, and eventually retire Intercom entirely.
- Thesis: a standardized, very flexible third-party tool becomes harder to manage at scale than a focused, AI-first build of their own.

**The acquisition (material, in progress):**
- Musixmatch is "in the middle of a possible acquisition."
- Logic: acquire for the asset / the work the target already did, then run operations with roughly **one third of the people** (or fewer). They cite confidence because they've already absorbed companies and improved efficiency this way.
- Implication for the hire: from day one there will be growing demand to answer, and potentially the loss of the people who used to handle it — so the AI system must come online fast. Treated as a *product rollout*: ~month 1 transition, ~3 months next stage taking more of the system, ~6 months tools possibly fully deprecated/replaced.

**Business model & the expansion bet:**
- Musixmatch pays rights holders — **composers/authors for both melody and lyrics**.
- Today the productized, managed "digital piece" is **the lyrics**. The strategic expansion is to handle the **"full piece"** (melody + lyrics, i.e., the full composition), capturing a larger share of the rights/royalty pie.
- This expansion requires scaling **managed (high-touch B2B) clients** — and those big relationships (e.g., **Sony, Warner**) are inherently human ("very much older"/traditional), which is *why* upskilling people for the human side matters even as the long tail is automated.
